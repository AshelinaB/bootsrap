Mission

Goodwill Industries of Greater New York and Northern New Jersey's community based services enable people with disabilities, new immigrants and refugees, veterans and their families, ex-offenders, the unemployed and underemployed, mature workers, and underserved youth the opportunity to fully participate in society.
We are known for moving people from welfare to work, but Goodwill empowered 95,000 individuals in 2014, through a wide array of services.
Partnering with local schools and businesses Goodwill provides opportunity for individuals with barriers, to grow and achieve their full potential, by teaching and cultivating life skills. Learn more about what Goodwill is doing in communities across New York and New Jersey.

Values

RECOGNITION that the people we serve are at the center of our mission, coming first in everything we do. RESPECT for the individuals we serve and their right of choice. ADHERENCE to the highest ethical standards, fiscal responsibility and efficient use of resources. EMPOWERMENT of individuals through innovative and quality services


About Us

We at Goodwill Industries will only be satisfied when every person in the communities we serve has the opportunity to achieve full potential as an individual and to participate and contribute in all aspects of a productive life.
Building strong communities is at the foundation of Goodwill's mission. When employers value diverse abilities everyone can contribute to society in their own way. We strive to create workplaces where each person is respected for their unique contributions.
Goodwill stores encourage efficient use of resources. By recovering usable items, in 2015 Goodwill diverted 117 million pounds from local landfills, up from 112 million pounds in 2014, 87 million pounds in 2013! Our focus on re-purposing and recycling promotes environmental sustainability and provides green jobs.
